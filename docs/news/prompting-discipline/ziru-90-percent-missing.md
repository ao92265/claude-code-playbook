# Why 90% of Claude Code Users Are Missing Its Most Powerful Feature

**Source:** ZIRU, cited in Medium Daily Digest of 2026-04-07 (Email 23). Direct URL: `medium.com/@jh.baek.sd/why-90-of-claude-code-users-are-missing-its-most-powerful-feature-52ef453785ed` (12 min read, 50 claps)

## Key takeaways

- Framing: **the secret isn't better prompts or smarter models** — it's the **harness/workflow layer** most users ignore.
- Lines up with the wider "Agent Harness" vocabulary Joe Njenga and Rick Hightower also use.
- Useful for convincing skeptical engineers that the harness *is* the product.
- Pair with [Agent Harness]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/news/agent-harness/) and [Superpowers / Cialdini]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/news/superpowers-cialdini/) for the full story.

## The argument

ZIRU's piece sits alongside Njenga's "Agent Harness" article as part of the April 2026 convergence on what the community is finally calling out: **you cannot prompt-engineer your way past a poorly-configured harness.** CLAUDE.md, hooks, skills, MCP servers — the layer *between* you and the model is where 90% of adoption is stuck.

## Why the framing works internally

When advocating for harness investment inside your team, this article is useful rhetorical support — "90% are missing it" is a compelling frame for allocating engineering time to CLAUDE.md hardening and skill library curation.
