# A Lawyer Just Beat 500 Developers at Anthropic's Hackathon

**Source:** Jing Hui in AI Advances, cited in Medium Daily Digest of 2026-04-07 (Email 23). 7 min read, 3.8K claps.

## Key takeaways

- **The most valuable skill in AI right now isn't code** — it's domain expertise paired with effective AI orchestration.
- A non-programmer (lawyer) beat 500 developers at Anthropic's own hackathon using Claude + domain knowledge.
- Signal for where AI-development value is concentrating: **domain × prompt craft > raw engineering**.

## Why this matters for Harris / Constellation

Many Constellation verticals have deep domain experts (banking, public sector, utilities, healthcare) who aren't traditional developers. The lawyer-beats-developers story validates that your highest-leverage AI investment may be **enabling your domain experts**, not just your engineering teams.

Concrete implications:
- Bundle CLAUDE.md templates and guided workflows for non-engineering teams
- Invest in "reverse prompting" skills (see [Prompt Patterns]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/prompt-patterns/)) for people without coding background
- Expect the most useful internal AI champions may not be on engineering
